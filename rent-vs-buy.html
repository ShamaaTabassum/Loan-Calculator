How to use our rent vs. buy calculator

If you're weighing the decision between renting and buying a house, our rent vs. buy calculator is a valuable tool. It allows you to analyze and compare expenses associated with homeownership and renting over a specific period, helping you determine if you're financially prepared for owning a home or if it's best to delay the purchase.

You’ll only need four pieces of information to get started:

  1. Your current ZIP code (or the ZIP code you’re thinking of buying in)
  2. Your desired home price Range
  3. Your target rent payment
  4. How long you plan to stay in the home
  5. After you’ve entered this information, the calculator will give you a buy or rent recommendation.

What a buy recommendation means

Based on the duration of your intended stay, our calculator will suggest purchasing a home if the costs of owning are lower than renting. Generally, the longer you plan to reside in a property, the more advantageous it becomes to buy rather than rent.

What a rent recommendation means

In case the expenses of purchasing a home exceed the costs of renting it for your intended duration of stay, our calculator will likely advise you to "rent." Moreover, if you are in the military or anticipate a potential relocation (due to employment or preferable climate conditions) in the near future, the financial implications of buying and subsequently selling a home may not be justified.

Our rent vs. buy calculator’s “advanced options:” What you should know

The calculator makes some assumptions about your down payment that are important to know to avoid any surprises. Here’s a quick look under the hood of the “advanced options” section:

Home buying

This section allows you to customize the calculations of the rent vs. buy calculator based on various home buying factors, including the following:

Down payment. The calculator assumes a default 20% down payment, but feel free to modify it if you have a different amount saved up (considering there are home loan programs with minimum down payments ranging from 0% to 3.5%). Additionally, for the most accurate recommendation, please check current mortgage rates for the interest rate input.

Mortgage term. Opting for a 30-year term provides the advantage of a lower monthly payment, while selecting a 15-year term is advisable if you can comfortably manage the higher payment associated with a shorter term.

Property taxes and home insurance. The calculator will provide an estimation of property taxes and homeowners insurance by considering common costs for your ZIP code. However, if you have precise figures for a specific house, you can enter them here for more accurate results.

HOA fees. In the case of purchasing a home within a homeowners association-controlled subdivision, please input the monthly dues associated with it.

Annual maintenance. As a homeowner, it is your responsibility to cover maintenance and repair costs. A general guideline is to allocate approximately 1% of your loan amount per year towards expenses related to upkeep or necessary repairs.

Annual home appreciation. By default, the calculator assumes a yearly appreciation rate of 3% for your house. However, it is important to note that this figure can vary significantly based on the conditions of your local real estate market, which may result in higher or lower appreciation rates.

Cost of selling your home. A disadvantage of homeownership is the customary payment of the full 6% real estate commission to agents involved in marketing and assisting you in finding a buyer for your home.

Closing costs. On average, you can expect to allocate approximately 2% of the closing costs towards expenses such as title fees, inspections, staging fees, and other costs associated with selling your home. However, please note that the exact amount may vary based on the preparations required to get your home ready for sale.

Investment return rate. The investment return rate assumed by the calculator is 3%, although this figure may vary depending on the real estate market conditions in your area.

Inflation. In our calculator, a 2% inflation rate is assumed. Low inflation is advantageous as it helps maintain low interest rates and increases your purchasing capacity. Conversely, high inflation can strain your budget and decrease affordability when it comes to homeownership.

Tax options

Homeownership offers several tax benefits, and if you have your tax information readily available, you can input that data into the respective fields of the rent vs. buy calculator below:

Annual income. This refers to your pre-tax income, which should encompass the combined earnings of all individuals involved in the homebuying application.

Marginal income tax rate. Consult your tax professional for assistance if you are unsure about the answer based on your recent tax returns.

Average savings and loan tax rate. This field compares the advantage of keeping your down payment funds in a savings account versus investing them in a home. The default value is set to 5%.

Other tax deductions. Enter any other tax deductions you usually claim in this field. The calculator assumes a minimum of $2,500 in additional deductions.

Married box. Selecting the "married" option informs the computer to determine the appropriate standard deductions based on your marital status.

Renting info

This section focuses on rental costs and allows you to tweak the following fields:

Rental inflation. seek advice from a local real estate agent specializing in home rentals. If unsure, the calculator assumes a 3% yearly increase in rent.

Annual rental insurance. Enter the amount of premium paid for rental insurance in this field. If left blank, the default value will be set to $180 per year.

Pros and cons of buying vs. renting a home

If you get a “buying” recommendation from the calculator, before you apply for a mortgage , take some time to mull over the pros and cons of buying versus renting a home:

Pros of buying a home Cons of owning a home
You build home equity with each mortgage payment You’re responsible for upkeep and repairs to your home
You can take advantage of tax benefits You could lose money if you have to sell the home due to financial hardship
You can improve and renovate the home however you wish Your home's value could drop
